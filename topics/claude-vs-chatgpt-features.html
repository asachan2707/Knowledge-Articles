8 Crazy Things Claude AI Can Do (That ChatGPT Can’t)

A Medium article from the No Time publication that goes feature-by-feature through where Claude’s product design pulls ahead of ChatGPT in 2026 — large-context work, design artifacts, in-chat apps, file system control, and integrated workflows like Slack and Projects.

Author Pranit Naik

Focus Practical Claude features missing from ChatGPT

The article is not a generic “Claude vs ChatGPT” review. It is specifically about capabilities that change how you work — features that push Claude from “a chatbot you ask questions” to “a workspace tool that produces shippable artifacts.” The differentiators are mostly about scope, integration, and output fidelity.

Core idea: Claude has quietly become more of a workspace than a chatbot, and several of its features simply do not have a ChatGPT equivalent yet.

Story · The 200K-token moment

The article opens with a simple before/after: a consultant trying to synthesize a 90-page research dossier. On ChatGPT, the dossier was chopped into 4 chunks, summarized in passes, and then re-stitched — losing fidelity at every step. On Claude, the entire dossier went into one conversation and produced a coherent executive summary in a single pass.

That moment is what made the author stop comparing models on benchmarks and start comparing them on workflows. The rest of the article is variations of the same realization.

Detailed breakdown

The 8 capabilities the article highlights

1. Real design artifacts, not just images

Claude can produce actual design assets — prototypes, slide decks, mockups, landing pages — that you can export to Canva, PDF, PowerPoint, or HTML. ChatGPT and DALL·E are great at generating images, but they do not give you a structured artifact you can hand to a designer or PM and immediately edit.

2. Interactive apps inside the conversation

Through Artifacts, Claude can build small working apps you can test inline — calculators, mini-tools, dashboards, prototypes — instead of just printing code you have to copy, paste, and run. This collapses the loop between “describe an idea” and “try the idea.”

3. Massive context handling

Claude’s 200K-token context window lets you load very long documents, multiple PDFs, large codebases, and full research dossiers into a single conversation. The article emphasizes that this is not just a number on a spec sheet — it changes the workflow. You can synthesize across documents instead of breaking work into fragments.

4. Slack as a native surface

Claude integrates directly into Slack as a quiet collaborator: summarizing threads, drafting responses, extracting action items, and answering questions in-channel. The point is reduced context switching — you stay where the work happens instead of bouncing to a separate tab.

5. File system access on your computer

With the right setup, Claude can create, read, and edit files and folders on your machine. That turns it from a text generator into something closer to an assistant that operates on your real workspace, not a sandbox copy of it.

6. Projects with persistent context

Claude’s Projects feature lets you upload reference documents, attach custom instructions, and have all of that automatically apply to every conversation in that project. You stop re-explaining your codebase, your brand voice, or your domain on every new chat.

7. Better factuality and tone in formal work

The article reports fewer hallucinations on formal content like reports, contracts, and analyses, and a more naturally professional tone by default. For business and writing-heavy use cases, this means less editing to make output sound serious.

8. Skills: reusable instruction packs

Skills let you wrap repeatable tasks (presentations, brand-aligned content, internal reports) into installable capability bundles. Claude invokes the right Skill when the task matches, which gives you deterministic, on-brand outputs without re-prompting every time.

When ChatGPT still wins

Honest framing matters

The article is bullish on Claude but it is not a takedown of ChatGPT. ChatGPT still has a stronger ecosystem of GPTs, broader plugin/extension presence, image generation maturity through DALL·E, and deeper voice mode capabilities. For multimodal creative work and the largest plugin marketplace, ChatGPT remains very strong.

The point is that the two systems are diverging by design. Claude is leaning into long-context reasoning, artifact generation, and integrated workflows. ChatGPT is leaning into multimodal interaction, ecosystem breadth, and consumer surface area.

Practical learning

How to choose between them for a task

  • Long documents and codebases: Claude — its context window changes the workflow.
  • Brand-consistent or repeatable artifacts: Claude with Projects + Skills.
  • Interactive prototypes inline: Claude Artifacts.
  • Image generation and creative visuals: ChatGPT with DALL·E.
  • Voice or multimodal conversations: ChatGPT advanced voice mode.
  • Slack-embedded team work: Claude integration.
  • Plugin/extension ecosystem: ChatGPT GPTs.

Common questions

FAQ for teams choosing between them

  • Should we standardize on one across the team?

    Most teams settle on one as the default for documentation/long-context work and keep the other for specific use cases (image generation, voice). A "one tool fits all" mandate usually leaves productivity on the table.

  • Is the safety/factuality difference real?

    Both models hallucinate. The article's claim is that Claude tends to over-hedge less in formal writing and more often defaults to a measured tone. Either way, citation grounding (RAG, retrieval, links) is what actually moves the reliability needle.

  • Will ChatGPT close these gaps soon?

    Likely, partly. OpenAI is shipping fast on context windows and tool use. The choices will look more similar over time, but product surface differences (Artifacts, Projects, Slack) take longer to replicate than raw model improvements.

Key Takeaway

The most useful framing in 2026 is not “which model is smarter,” but “which surface fits this job.” Claude has clearly invested in long-context reasoning, real artifacts, persistent project context, and workflow integration. ChatGPT has invested in multimodal interaction and ecosystem reach. Use the one whose differentiators match the work in front of you.
