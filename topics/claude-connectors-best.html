The 10 Best Claude Connectors — And Why You Need Them

Claude's problem is not prompt quality — it is context. Connectors solve this by letting Claude directly access the apps where your work actually lives, instead of relying on copy-paste. Here are the ten connectors that change how you work.

SourceFrank Andrade & Kevin Gargate Osorio — Artificial Corner, May 2026
InstallClaude web: Customize → Connectors · Claude Code: MCP config

Skills tell Claude how to work. Connectors give Claude access to where the work actually lives. Without connectors, every Claude interaction starts from scratch — you paste content in, Claude responds, you copy back. Connectors collapse that loop.

The shift: Instead of "Here is the Google Doc I want you to summarise — [paste 3,000 words]", you say "Summarise the product brief in my Drive" and Claude reads it directly. Same result, fraction of the friction.

The ten connectors

Docs & Knowledge

1. Google Drive

Search and read files across your Drive without leaving Claude. Summarise long documents, extract action items from meeting notes, compare two versions of a brief. Most useful for anyone who stores reference material in Drive and wants Claude to use it without manual pasting.

2. Notion

Search pages by keyword and reorganise content across your workspace. Useful for knowledge bases, project wikis, and task tracking. Ask Claude to find all pages related to a topic, then synthesise them — without opening Notion or copying anything.

Communication

3. Gmail

Search email threads and get summaries of conversations. Most valuable use case: "Summarise everything in my inbox related to [project]" or "What did I agree to in the last 48 hours?" Claude reads the threads, not you.

4. Google Calendar

Access your real schedule. Ask Claude to plan your week, identify conflicts, suggest when to schedule a meeting, or summarise upcoming commitments. Unlike asking Claude about scheduling in general, this connector works from your actual calendar data.

5. Slack

Search threads and summarise channel activity. The primary use cases: "Catch me up on the #product channel since last Wednesday" and "Find all messages mentioning [decision]." Replaces the painful experience of scrolling through Slack history to reconstruct context.

Design

6. Figma

Access frames and components directly for code generation. Eliminates the cycle of exporting, describing, and approximating design specs. Claude sees the actual component hierarchy and generates code from it. Essential if any of your work involves translating design to code.

7. Canva

Merges ideation and design. Describe what you want, and Claude can work with Canva's API to draft visual layouts. Most useful for non-designers who need functional output quickly.

Code & Data

8. GitHub

Read files, review PRs in context, and understand code changes without switching tabs. Claude can compare branches, summarise a PR's purpose, or identify what changed between two commits — directly from the repository. Combined with Claude Code, this makes code review significantly faster.

9. BigQuery

Review schema and generate SQL queries in natural language. Describe the analysis you need; Claude reads the schema and produces the query. No more writing SQL from memory or copy-pasting schema definitions to give Claude context.

Business Operations

10. Stripe

Access customer records and payment history. Ask questions about revenue, subscriptions, churn, or specific customers without exporting to spreadsheets first. Most valuable for founders and operators who need fast answers from payment data.

Installation

Two ways to install connectors

Claude web (claude.ai)

Navigate to Customize → Connectors. Each connector requires authorising access to the relevant service via OAuth. Takes 2–3 minutes per connector.

Claude Code (CLI)

Most connectors are available as MCP servers. Configure in your ~/.claude/settings.json under the mcpServers key. The GitHub and BigQuery connectors in particular are most useful in Claude Code where you are already in a development context.

Key Takeaway

The bottleneck for most Claude users is not intelligence — it is context. Every manual copy-paste is friction that makes Claude less useful and you less likely to use it for complex tasks. Start with the two or three connectors that cover where your work actually lives (probably Drive or Notion plus Slack or Gmail) and add from there. The workflow shift from copy-paste to direct access compounds quickly.
