The opening observation is the kind that bothers people enough to act: developers with the same skill set, same years of experience, earning 2x more, while never sending a single application. Story · Same resume, 2x salary

The writer noticed peers with identical CVs earning twice as much. The only variable: they were getting reached out to, not applying.

That gap is the entire article. Visibility, narrative, and curated proof of work flip the dynamic of who chases whom.

Detailed breakdown

Key insights and why they matter

1) Public job boards are the loudest, lowest-paying lane

Tens of thousands of applicants per high-signal role. Compensation is bid down by volume, not skill.

2) Talent networks are the hidden inbound layer

Vetted talent pools (Toptal, Arc, Lemon.io, Gun.io, A.Team, niche Slack/Discord communities) match curated developers to inbound roles.

3) Public proof of work changes the math

A handful of public projects, technical writeups, or open-source contributions raise your perceived signal beyond CV alone.

4) Headline + niche = inbound DMs

A LinkedIn headline that names a domain ("Python + payments + AWS") outperforms generic "full-stack developer" for inbound recruiter signal.

5) Compensation comes from leverage, not request

2-3 active conversations in parallel give you negotiation leverage. One offer is a request; three offers is a market.

Practical learning

How to apply this in real work

  • Rewrite LinkedIn headline as "domain + stack + outcome" (e.g., "Backend engineer · Python + AWS · ML infrastructure").
  • Publish 2-3 small but real public projects with READMEs explaining the why.
  • Apply to 1-2 vetted talent networks this quarter.
  • Keep 2-3 conversations alive at any time — even when you are not actively job hunting.
Common questions

FAQ

  • How long until inbound starts?

    Usually 2-4 months of consistent visibility before recruiters start finding you reliably.

  • Do I still need to apply?

    Yes — applications and inbound are complementary. Apply for the role you want; let inbound surface roles you would not have found.

  • Is this only for senior engineers?

    No. The same playbook works at mid-level, just with smaller compensation deltas at first.
